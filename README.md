# 🧑‍💼 Employee Attrition Analysis : O-Zone Telecom



## 🧑‍💼 Employee Attrition Analysis – O-Zone Telecom

This project analyzes employee attrition and well-being at O-Zone Telecom to identify the main factors influencing turnover and absenteeism. Using HR data and machine learning techniques in Python, the analysis combines Exploratory Data Analysis, PCA, K-means clustering, and Logistic Regression. Findings highlight strong relationships between attrition, working conditions, job satisfaction, and career progression opportunities. Based on these findings, I recommend the following actions to improve employee retention:

1. Improve work-life balance by reducing excessive overtime and travel requirements.
2. Promote career development through structured promotion pathways.
3. Implement targeted HR strategies tailored to different employee profiles.

### Business Problem:

O-Zone Telecom is expanding its activities in Africa and faces high absenteeism and employee turnover rates. These issues complicate talent retention and limit the company's ability to develop in emerging markets. Conflicting opinions within the HR team exist regarding the main drivers of attrition, particularly between gender disparities and working conditions.

This project explores which factors most influence employee attrition and how data-driven insights can guide HR policies to improve retention.

### Methodology:

1. Data Collection & Preprocessing: Used an employee HR dataset containing demographic, professional, and satisfaction-related variables; removed irrelevant variables and prepared the dataset for analysis.
2. Exploratory Analysis & Visualization: Analyzed variable distributions, correlations, and summary statistics to understand employee characteristics and attrition patterns.
3. Dimensionality Reduction: Applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce the number of variables from 41 to 14 while preserving most of the information.
4. Clustering: Applied K-means clustering to identify distinct employee profiles and understand attrition patterns across groups.
5. Predictive Modeling: Built logistic regression models on the full dataset and on each cluster to determine the factors influencing employee attrition.

### Skills:

1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing: Handling missing values, selecting relevant features, and preparing structured datasets.
2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Analyzing distributions, correlations, and key indicators to identify patterns.
3. Machine Learning & Modeling: Applying PCA, clustering algorithms, and logistic regression models.
4. Data Visualization: Communicating insights using clear and meaningful visualizations.
5. Python Programming & Tools: Using Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn, and Jupyter Notebooks.

### Results & Business Recommendation:

This study identifies several important factors influencing employee attrition. Excessive overtime strongly increases attrition by negatively affecting work-life balance. Limited career growth opportunities and lack of promotion also significantly contribute to employee dissatisfaction. Job satisfaction, environmental satisfaction, and job involvement play major roles in employee retention.

Three main employee profiles were identified:

Stable Veterans: Long-tenured employees with low attrition risk but limited career growth.
Performance Seekers: Mid-level employees motivated by compensation and performance recognition.
At-Risk Newcomers: Recently hired employees with higher attrition risk due to dissatisfaction and workload stress.

I recommend these actions to ensure that HR policies effectively improve retention and employee well-being:

1. Improving work-life balance through flexible work arrangements.
2. Developing personalized career growth pathways.
3. Providing competitive compensation and recognition programs.
4. Reducing excessive workloads and travel requirements.
5. Implementing targeted retention strategies for different employee profiles.
